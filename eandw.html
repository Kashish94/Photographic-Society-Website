WorkShops and Tutorials

WORKSHOPS

BASIC PHOTOGRAPHY

These workshops are aimed to impart basic knowledge about photography to enthusiasts; teaching them about the structure and working of the camera, the aesthetics of a good photograph and introducing them to the concepts of shutter, aperture, ISO, exposure, white balance, etc. DSLR's are given to them in hand, where they try all the concepts taught to them. Both classroom and field workshops are conducted.

ADVANCED PHOTOGRAPHY

These workshops go into the intricacies of photography, the three modes of operating the camera, panning, ghosting, light painting, bokehs, etc. Umpteen field workshops are conducted for them to practice. Photowalks are organised in the campus to implement the same.

SCIENCE FILM MAKING WORKSHOP

Filmmaking is the process of making a film, generally in the sense of films intended for extensive theatrical exhibition. Filmmaking involves a number of discrete stages including an initial story, idea, or commission, through screenwriting, casting, shooting, sound recording and pre-production, editing, and screening the finished product before an audience that may result in a film release and exhibition.

GRAPHIC DESIGNING WORKSHOP

“A designer knows he has achieved perfection not when there is nothing left to add, but when there is nothing left to take away.” We have a bunch of people on our team proficient in Photoshop, Illustrator and Lightroom, who make mind blowing pieces of photo manipulation. They actively conduct workshops to teach the same.

TUTORIALS

BASIC DESIGNING TUTORIALS

As we further delve into the futuristic realm of modernization, hand rendered media are slowly transformed into digital art. If you have interest in graphic design but never took this forward, well now is the time to learn something big. We have got you a go-to resource for all your design inspiration. In the first installment, get to learn about the necessary tools and essential tips needed to tackle your graphic designing ambitions.

CAMERA LENS TUTORIALS

Lenses are arguably the most important part of your camera set-up, they make or break your pictures. They control the image that's projected onto your imaging sensor, and ultimately what photos you are taking home. To the uninitiated, lenses are baffling tubes of glass with numbers and confusing acronyms printed on the side.
